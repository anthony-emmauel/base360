# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

DTC/social-commerce sellers and small brand teams drowning in inbound comments and DMs across TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp, SMS, and email. They're evaluating Base360 while mentally toggling between too many inboxes, worried leads are slipping through the cracks. The job to be done: stop losing sales to unanswered comments and DMs, without hiring more people to triage them.

## Product Purpose

Base360 unifies every conversation across social/DM/SMS/email into one inbox, automates the busywork (replies, qualification, hand-off), and turns leads into closed sales automatically. Success for this page: a visitor understands in one scroll that "a comment becomes a customer with zero manual effort" and starts a free trial.

## Brand Personality

Confident, blunt, fast. Copy is direct and unpadded ("One comment. Closed customer. Zero effort.") — no generic SaaS filler, no hedging. The product proves itself by showing real UI (the inbox/thread panel), not by stacking marketing adjectives.

References and what fits from each: Linear, Vercel, Raycast, n8n, Mintlify — dark UI, one restrained accent color, developer-tool-grade precision and typographic discipline. Intercom, respond.io, Fyxer, Granola — the inbox/support-tool feel and the pattern of letting a live embedded product view do the persuading.

## Anti-references

Generic glossy SaaS/DTC marketing: stock-gradient heroes, oversized rounded cards, decorative padding that isn't earning its place. If a section could be mistaken for a template DTC-tool landing page, it's failed.

Stock photography is permitted where it depicts the actual DTC-seller audience in a specific, physical moment (checking a phone, on a call, at a laptop) — not generic "team in a boardroom" corporate photography. When used, it should pair with a real product-UI overlay so the product still does the proving, not the photo alone.

## Design Principles

- **Confidence over decoration** — direct copy, precise type, nothing added just to fill space.
- **Product-as-proof** — the embedded inbox/thread panel carries the persuasion; marketing copy stays out of its way.
- **Technical-grade restraint** — dark surface, one accent color, developer-tool-lineage polish (Linear/Vercel/Raycast), not DTC gloss.
- **Speed as a felt experience** — motion and copy both signal "zero effort"; no friction, no unnecessary steps.
- **Every claim earns its place** — blunt, specific copy; cut anything a generic SaaS page could also say.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, full keyboard support, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all motion (already respected by the existing thread-line animation).
